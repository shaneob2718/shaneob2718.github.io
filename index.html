About Me

I'm Shane O'Brien, a recent physics graduate from the University of California, Irvine. My main area of research interest is observational astrophysics, speciffically in exoplanet astronomy. I am currently working with the Robertson Research Group at UC Irvine and the GEMS (Gian Exoplanets around M-dwarf Stars) Collaboration in confirming TOI-5916b and TOI-6158b as exoplanets in hopes to better understand how these rare GEMS systems form. In addition to this work, I also have interest in experiemental high energy physics, where I did some minor research in this area during my undergrad as well.

When I am not doing physics, I enjoy going for long walks, practicing my Chess skills, reading, playing video games, and working on personal programming projects.
